# Investor Disclosures

> Not legal advice. This disclosure text must be reviewed and approved by legal counsel before mainnet launch.

## Purpose

Provide investor-facing risk and product disclosures for fundraising, equity token claims, and profit distribution.

## Core Risk Statement

Investments in tokenized real-estate offerings are speculative and can result in partial or total loss of capital.

## Required Disclosures

### 1) No Guaranteed Returns

- Returns are not guaranteed.
- Historical performance does not predict future performance.
- Profit distributions depend on underlying property performance and costs.

### 2) Loss Of Principal

- Investors may lose some or all invested funds.
- Campaign failure or operational issues may affect outcomes.

### 3) Liquidity Risk

- Equity tokens may be illiquid.
- Secondary markets may be unavailable or have limited depth.
- Exit timing and pricing may be uncertain.

### 4) Smart Contract And Technical Risk

- Smart contracts and infrastructure may contain bugs, vulnerabilities, or integration failures.
- RPC/provider issues or chain instability can delay or disrupt operations.
- Wallet compromise can cause irreversible asset loss.

### 5) Regulatory And Jurisdiction Risk

- Availability depends on jurisdiction and investor eligibility.
- Regulatory interpretation may change and impact access, transferability, or product structure.

### 6) Operational And Counterparty Risk

- Platform, operator, or third-party failures may delay or prevent expected workflows.
- Offchain dependencies (servicers, banking rails, legal entities) can affect outcomes.

### 7) Fee And Cost Transparency

- Platform and transaction fees reduce net investor outcomes.
- Gas/network costs are borne by users unless explicitly sponsored.

## Investor Acknowledgement Requirements

Before enabling production investment actions, the product must capture acknowledgement that users understand:

1. The risk of partial or total loss.
2. No guaranteed returns or liquidity.
3. Technical and smart-contract risks.
4. Regulatory restrictions may apply.

## Product Placement Requirements

Disclosures must be visible in:

1. Home/onboarding context.
2. Property invest flow before transaction submission.
3. Owner/operator surfaces that can influence investor outcomes.

## Change Control

- Disclosure versions must be dated and versioned.
- Material changes require legal re-approval before release.
